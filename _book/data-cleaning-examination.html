3.3 Data Cleaning / Examination

Let’s begin this part of our workflow by reading in our competition’s data into our current R session:

modelData <- read_csv("train(1).csv")
testData <- read_csv("test(1).csv")

Do note the following at this point:

  1. modelData is a variable that will host our competition’s training data. That is, we will be using this .csv file (and only this .csv file) to build our models and perform exploratory data analysis.

    It is imperative that we don’t including testing data at this point of our workflow - if we did, then although our model will perform very well in the end, it would be very prone to overfitting and would likely perform badly on other unseen data.

  2. testData is our testing set. Once we are happy with our predictive model’s performance, we will apply it to testData and submit our predictions off to Kaggle to be evaluated.

According to Kaggle, the variables’ meanings are as follows:

  1. PassengerId - this is a unique identifier for each passenger.
  2. Survived- did the passenger survive (0 = died, 1 = survived)?
  3. Pclass - the passenger’s ticket class.
  4. Name - the passenger’s name.
  5. Sex - the passenger’s biological gender.
  6. Age - the passenger’s age.
  7. SibSp - the amount of siblings and / or spouses the passenger has on board the Titanic.
  8. Parch - the amount of parents and / or children the passenger has on board the Titanic.
  9. Ticket - the passenger’s ticket number.
  10. Fare - the passenger’s ticket fare.
  11. Cabin - the passenger’s cabin number.
  12. Embarked - from which port did the passenger board the Titanic (S = Southampton, C = Cherbourg, Q = Queenstown)?

Furthermore, I also see some NAs present in some of our variables - just how many NAs are there in both modelData’s and testData’s variables?

Based on the above codeblocks (and Kaggle’s description of the variables that we have to work with), I noted the following:

  1. Our categorical variables (i.e., Sex, Pclass, and Embarked) are not factors like they should be.
  2. We have tons of NAs in the Cabin variable of both modelData and testData. I think we should discard this variable from both sets of data as a significant chunk (i.e., more than 77% of our variable’s data) is missing.
  3. A good chunk of our passengers’ ages also appear to be missing. We’re going to need to find something to impute those missing ages with if we don’t want to omit these passengers altogether.
  4. We also have some missing data in some of the other variables. While we could impute these pieces of missing data with something, we could also remove these passengers or leave them be (depending on the variables that we do end up using to train our models).
  5. PassengerId in modelData is useless and should be removed.

So, let’s get started with 1., 2., and 5. first:

modelData$Survived <- as.factor(modelData$Survived)
modelData$Sex <- as.factor(modelData$Sex)
modelData$Embarked <- as.factor(modelData$Embarked)
modelData$Pclass <- as.factor(modelData$Pclass)
